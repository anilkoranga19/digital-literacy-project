UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. This type of fraud has increased rapidly with the growth of digital payments.
Typically, the fraud happens in several steps. First, the attacker contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or messaging apps, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer/seller. Next, they create urgency by claiming issues like account blocking, refund processing, or prize winnings. Then, they send a payment request or a malicious link and instruct the victim to “approve” it. Many victims mistakenly believe they are receiving money, but in reality, they are authorizing a payment. In some cases, fraudsters also ask for OTPs or PINs.
The primary targets are students, elderly people, and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment security. Small business owners are also frequent victims.
The consequences can be severe, including immediate financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering the money. In many cases, victims may not get refunds due to delayed reporting.
To prevent such fraud, users should never share OTPs or PINs, avoid clicking unknown links, and carefully read payment requests before approving them. Awareness and caution are the best defenses against UPI fraud.
